About Me

My name is Quan (pronounced somewhat like "tron" in Megatron). I moved to Chicago in 2010 from China to pursue a PhD degree in biology/biomedical sciences at the Unviversity of Chicago. You can find my graduate work in finding a cure for a devastating muscle wasting disease highlighted here.

Years past, degree obtained, I carried on research work but switched disease of interest to cancer. You can find my newest work into cancer "assassin" molecules here. The field of biomedical sciences is booming with big data and the need to understand it. Ever since I taught myself R for experimental analysis, I got interested in delving deeper into the programming world. I like executing logic, identifying rules, fixing problems (even if it's that stupid office printer), and building things.
